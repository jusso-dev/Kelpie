# Kelpie Product Context

## Register

product

## Users

Kelpie serves analysts and administrators in small security operations teams. Analysts use it repeatedly during active investigations, often under time pressure, to triage alerts, coordinate response work, preserve evidence, and move incidents to closure. Administrators configure the self-hosted workspace, integrations, access, and operational policy.

## Product Purpose

Kelpie is an open-source, self-hosted SOC case-management workbench. It keeps alerts, cases, observables, tasks, response actions, comments, and audit history connected so a small team can understand what needs attention, act safely, and account for every decision. Success means less duplicated triage, fewer missed response steps, faster incident resolution, and a trustworthy record of what happened.

## Brand Personality

Focused, trustworthy, and quietly technical. The product voice is calm, direct, specific, and operational. It uses Australian spelling and avoids jokes or alarmist language in incident workflows.

## Anti-references

Kelpie must not resemble a generic admin template, a marketing site inside the authenticated product, a playful consumer app, or a neon cyberpunk security toy. Avoid decorative dashboards, excessive cards, gradient-heavy surfaces, novelty controls, and animation that competes with incident data.

## Design Principles

1. Put urgent work first. A glance should reveal what is critical, what changed, and what needs the analyst now.
2. Make operational data scannable. Case identifiers, severity, ownership, due state, SLA pressure, observables, and timestamps must remain legible at high density.
3. Make dangerous actions deliberate. State consequences, name the target, and preserve an audit trail.
4. Keep the tool predictable. Familiar controls and consistent information architecture let trained analysts stay in flow.
5. Explain failure and recovery. Errors should state what happened, what remains safe, and the next useful action without exposing secrets.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for web surfaces. Support keyboard-only operation, visible focus, screen readers, zoom and reflow, reduced motion, and high-contrast use. Never communicate severity or status by colour alone. Interactive targets should remain usable on touch devices, and dense data must have a narrow-screen alternative or clearly signposted overflow.
